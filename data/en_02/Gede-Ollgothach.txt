Gede Ollgothach, son of Ollom Fotla, was, according to medieval Irish legend and historical tradition, a High King of Ireland. He succeeded to the throne on the death of his brother Slanoll. His epithet means "possessing a great voice", and the Lebor Gabala Erenn says during his reign all his subjects had voices as sweet as the strings of a zither. He ruled for eight,R. A. Stewart Macalister (ed. & trans.), Lebor Gabala Erenn: The Book of the Taking of Ireland Part V, Irish Texts Society, 1956, pp. 237 or twelve,Annals of the Four Masters 3959-3971 or seventeen years,Geoffrey Keating, Foras Feasa ar Eirinn 1.26 before being killed by, according to the Lebor Gabala, the otherwise unknown Fiachu son of Fiadchu; according to Geoffrey Keating and the Annals of the Four Masters, by his nephew and successor Fiachu Findoilches, son of Finnachta. The chronology of Keating's Foras Feasa ar Eirinn dates his reign to 880-863 BC, that of the Annals of the Four Masters to 1241-1231 BC.
