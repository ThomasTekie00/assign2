Finnachta, son of Ollom Fotla, was, according to medieval Irish legend and historical tradition, a High King of Ireland in succession to his father. His given name was Elim.Annals of the Four Masters M3922-3942 There is said to have been snow of wine (Old Irish fin, "wine", snechta, "snow")Dictionary of the Irish Language, Compact Edition, Royal Irish Academy, 1990, pp. 306, 552 in his reign, from which he gained his better-known name.
